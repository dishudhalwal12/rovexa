# Rovexa Homepage PRD

## Project Overview
Rovexa is a rebranded growth agency that helps brands grow through a combined toolkit of social media, creative branding, websites, tech, automations, operations, and distribution support. The homepage should introduce Rovexa as a warm, premium, human, cinematic, creative agency — not a cold automation company and not a tech-architecture themed studio.

This is the first phase of the website build. Only the homepage should be built now. The homepage should be strong enough to act as the MVP entry point for the brand, while hinting at future pages like About, Services, Work, and Contact.

## Primary Goal
Create a homepage that makes Rovexa feel:
- soft
- cinematic
- premium
- human
- creative
- modern
- playful in subtle ways

The site should feel like a high-end creative growth agency, with black-background editorial polish, emotionally resonant copy, and tasteful motion.

## Core Brand Definition
**One-line positioning:**
Rovexa is a growth agency that helps brands grow through social media, branding, websites, tech systems, operations, and distribution.

**Core promise:**
Your brand should grow.

**Primary CTA:**
Get an audit

## Services to Represent
These are final and must be reflected in the homepage structure and messaging:
1. Marketing
2. Creative Branding
3. Tech
4. Operations and Distribution

The homepage should not present these in a dry, corporate, or overly technical way. They should feel interconnected under the larger idea of growth.

## Brand Tone
The old website tone must be completely discarded. Do not use robotic, hacker, terminal, protocol, machine, architecture, or "system v2" language.

### Tone rules
The copy must feel like it was written by an excellent human copywriter.
It should be:
- warm
- intentional
- premium
- confident
- readable
- emotionally intelligent
- fun to read without becoming gimmicky

It must not feel like:
- AI slop
- generic agency filler
- startup jargon spam
- cyberpunk tech copy
- cold automation marketing

### Writing style guidance
- Use simple but elegant language.
- Favor short-to-medium sentences with rhythm.
- Avoid overexplaining.
- Avoid buzzwords unless absolutely necessary.
- Let the copy feel editorial, cinematic, and human.
- SEO should be naturally embedded, not stuffed.

## Design Direction
### Overall aesthetic
The visual reference direction should blend:
- dark cinematic websites
- Apple/MacBook Pro style black-and-white restraint
- Versal-inspired modern motion and polish
- subtle luxury
- playful but controlled visual moments

### Color palette
Use the user-approved palette:
- Background: black
- Primary text / light foreground: off-white `#FAF9F6`

Do not use the previously discussed yellow direction.

### Typography
Use these fonts in the design system:
- **Logo / signature type:** Migra Semi Bold
- **Headings:** DM Sans Bold
- **Subtext / body:** Inter Light

Typography should be one of the strongest parts of the homepage. It should feel expensive, minimal, and readable.

## Brand Symbolism and Motifs
The brand symbolism should be present.

### Flower motif
The flower should remain part of the brand world.
It can appear in icon form, motion moments, subtle decorative assets, or transitions.

### Cheetah identity
The cheetah story should influence the homepage. It should not feel childish or literal.
The cheetah should appear through tasteful visual motifs such as:
- subtle cheetah tail-inspired motion
- tasteful cheetah skin or print references
- looping GIF/video elements
- elegant abstract animal energy rather than obvious wildlife imagery

This symbolism should support the feeling of the brand rather than overpower it.

### Important note
The brand symbolism can be mentioned on the homepage, but it should be handled tastefully. It should not read like fantasy lore. It should feel like a real premium brand story.

## Homepage UX Goal
When a visitor lands on the homepage, they should quickly feel:
1. This brand is visually sharp and premium.
2. This team helps brands grow.
3. They do more than just one service.
4. They combine creative work with execution.
5. They feel modern, warm, and worth trusting.

## Homepage Structure
The homepage should be built section by section in this order.

### 1. Hero Section
#### Goal
Immediately establish Rovexa as a premium creative growth agency.

#### Requirements
- Black background
- White/off-white typography
- Cinematic feel
- Strong motion presence
- Subtle playful luxury
- One clear CTA: Get an audit
- Rich but restrained animation

#### Hero layout guidance
- Clean navigation
- High-impact headline
- Short supporting subtext
- Primary CTA button
- Optional secondary micro-link if needed, but not required
- Visual layer should include cinematic motion or looping assets

#### Hero visual direction
The hero should not look like a generic SaaS site.
Possible visual ingredients:
- tasteful cheetah-tail-inspired motion trails
- subtle cheetah-print influenced motion textures
- soft flower-inspired graphic assets
- dark editorial atmosphere
- smooth premium transitions
- layered motion that feels alive but not cluttered

The user likes dark cinematic references with good animated text.
Text animation should feel premium, not flashy.

### 2. Brand Positioning / Intro Section
This section should explain what Rovexa actually does in a clear and elegant way.
It should communicate that Rovexa helps brands grow using a modern toolkit that spans:
- social media
- branding
- websites
- tech systems
- operations
- distribution

This section should bridge emotion and clarity.

### 3. Services Section
Present the four final service pillars clearly:
- Marketing
- Creative Branding
- Tech
- Operations and Distribution

#### Requirements
- Visually premium cards or panels
- Not overly boxed-in if a more editorial layout works better
- Each service should feel like part of one ecosystem
- Copy should emphasize growth, not just execution

### 4. Why Rovexa / Philosophy Section
This section should explain the underlying belief:
Rovexa exists to help brands grow beautifully and strategically.

It should feel human and thoughtful, not abstract.
It can lightly tie into the flower/cheetah symbolism if done tastefully.

### 5. Process or Engagement Section
A light process section can help reduce confusion and improve conversion.
It should explain how working with Rovexa begins, ideally in a simple and premium format.

Possible flow:
- Audit
- Direction
- Build
- Grow

Keep it elegant and not corporate.

### 6. Closing CTA Section
A final strong section that invites users to take action.
Main CTA should remain:
**Get an audit**

This area should feel decisive, confident, and clean.

## Navigation
For the homepage MVP, navigation should hint at future site structure.
Suggested nav:
- Home
- About
- Services
- Work
- Contact

Primary navigation CTA: Get an audit

## Motion and Interaction Design
Motion should be used throughout the homepage, but with restraint.

### Motion style
- smooth
- cinematic
- premium
- soft
- slightly playful
- not over-animated

### Motion ideas
- text reveal animations
- fade-in with slight upward drift
- layered parallax in hero
- looping background or accent motion
- tasteful hover states
- micro-interactions that feel polished

### Avoid
- glitch effects
- hacker effects
- terminal UI
- excessive particle chaos
- overly aggressive transitions
- anything that feels cheap or template-driven

## SEO Direction
The homepage copy should naturally support discovery around topics such as:
- growth agency
- branding agency
- social media agency
- website design
- creative agency
- automation and operations support

However, SEO must be woven into natural language. Do not keyword-stuff.

## Copy Expectations
Codex should generate refined homepage copy from scratch.
Do not reuse or paraphrase the old Rovexa copy style.

### Copy should accomplish three things
1. Feel premium and human.
2. Clearly explain what Rovexa does.
3. Be enjoyable to read.

### Avoid these traits
- robotic
- overly techy
- pseudo-intellectual
- buzzword-heavy
- too much founder ego
- vague abstract manifesto writing

## Technical Build Guidance for Codex
Codex should build a clean, modern homepage from scratch.

### Build priorities
- desktop-first polish, but fully responsive
- smooth scroll experience
- high contrast readability
- performance-conscious motion
- reusable section structure for future expansion

### Design implementation notes
- keep the page visually premium and spacious
- use black as the dominant canvas
- use off-white `#FAF9F6` for main text and light surfaces
- honor the approved font stack: Migra Semi Bold, DM Sans Bold, Inter Light
- implement tasteful animated text in hero
- create room for future About, Services, Work, and Contact pages

## What Not to Build
Do not build:
- terminal-style layouts
- architecture-grid hacker visuals
- protocol/system language UI
- neon tech startup designs
- generic agency template sections with empty claims
- loud yellow UI direction

## Final Output for This Phase
Codex should deliver:
1. A complete homepage design and implementation
2. Fresh homepage copy written from scratch
3. Premium motion and interaction design
4. Clear structure for future website expansion
5. A homepage that feels like a real rebrand, not a remix of the old website

## Summary
Rovexa is being repositioned as a premium, soft, cinematic, human, creative growth agency.
The homepage should feel emotionally resonant, visually controlled, and modern.
It should communicate that Rovexa helps brands grow through marketing, creative branding, tech, and operations/distribution.
The site should use a black-and-off-white palette, approved typography, elegant motion, and tasteful flower/cheetah-inspired brand symbolism.
Everything should be rewritten from zero.
The final result should feel like a real high-end creative agency website, with copy and design strong enough to impress serious clients and feel enjoyable to read.
